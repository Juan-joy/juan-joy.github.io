Placed 2nd at ChemQuest 2025, a case competition organised by the NUS Chemical Engineering Students' Society (ChESS), sponsored by GF Piping Systems APAC and Abbott.

## The Brief

We were given a fictional pharmaceutical client, Vireon, whose piping infrastructure had reached end-of-life. Our task was to evaluate three candidate materials - carbon steel, PVC, and PE100, and recommend a full replacement solution backed by a technical report and live presentation.

## Process

The competition opened with a site visit to GF Piping Systems' APAC headquarters in Singapore, where we got hands-on exposure to each material and observed different welding techniques in practice. The final presentation was held at NUS College of Design and Engineering, judged by faculty from the ChBE department and senior personnel from GF.

## What I Applied

Fluid mechanics and heat transfer formed the technical backbone of our analysis. Beyond the engineering, the competition pushed me to weigh factors that textbooks gloss over — capital and operating expenditure, sustainability targets, and the practical realities of installation. It was one of the most grounded applications of my engineering education to date.
