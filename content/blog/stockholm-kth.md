In 2024, I moved to Stockholm for NUS Overseas Colleges, splitting my time between KTH Royal Institute of Technology, the Stockholm School of Economics, and a biotech startup called AAX Biotech. It was the most formative year of my university life, and not for the reasons I expected.

## The Startup

At AAX Biotech, I worked on product development of a bioinformatics tool - the actual, hands-dirty kind where you're in the lab iterating on formulations one week and writing investor decks the next. The team was small, the problems were real, and nobody cared what year of study I was in.

What I learned there couldn't have come from a textbook: how to scope a problem when there's no brief, how to make decisions with incomplete data, and how to stay useful when you're the least experienced person in the room.

## The Cold

Stockholm in winter is dark by 3pm. The adjustment wasn't just physical - it was psychological. I had to build routines, find community, and create structure in a place where I knew nobody. That experience taught me more about resilience than any group project ever did.

## What Stayed With Me

A bias toward action. A comfort with ambiguity. And the conviction that the best learning happens when you're slightly out of your depth.
